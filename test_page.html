Cardiovascular Health

A stroke occurs when there is a lack of blood to a portion of the brain, resulting in tissue death and loss of brain function. This can be caused by a ruptured blood vessel, such as an aneurysm, or by an embolism.

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how your body metabolizes glucose. There are two main types: Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes. Both can lead to serious complications if not properly managed.

Cancer Awareness

Breast cancer is one of the most common causes of cancer fatality in women. Early detection through regular breast self-examination and mammography is key to successful treatment.

Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer deaths among men and women. Cigarette smoking poses the greatest risk for developing lung cancer.

Eye Health

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often due to abnormally high pressure in the eye. It can lead to vision loss if not treated early.

Diabetic retinopathy is a diabetes complication that affects the eyes. It's caused by damage to the blood vessels of the light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye.

Digestive Health

Gallstones are hardened deposits of digestive fluid that can form in your gallbladder. They can cause severe pain and may require surgical removal.

Kidney stones are hard deposits of minerals and salts that form inside your kidneys. They can cause severe pain when they move through the urinary tract.
